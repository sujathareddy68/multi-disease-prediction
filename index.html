Diseases and Symptoms:

Diabetes

It is a disease that occurs when your blood glucose, also called blood sugar, is too high.
Blood glucose is your main source of energy and comes from the food you eat.
Insulin, a hormone made by the pancreas, helps glucose from food get
into your cells to be used for energy.

Symptoms of Diabetes:

  • Increased thirst and hunger
  • Frequent urination
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred vision

Breast Cancer

It is a cancer that forms in the cells of the breast. After skin cancer,
breast cancer is the most common cancer diagnosed in women in the US.
Breast cancer can occur in both men and women,
but it's far more common in women.

Symptoms of Breast Cancer:

  • Breast lump or thickening
  • Change in breast size or shape
  • Nipple discharge other than breast milk
  • Redness or pitting of the breast skin
  • Changes in the skin over the breast

Heart Disease

Heart disease, or cardiovascular disease,
refers to conditions affecting the heart and blood vessels,
often caused by factors like poor diet, smoking, and high blood pressure.

Symptoms of Heart Disease:

  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat
  • Dizziness or fainting

Liver Disease

Liver disease encompasses a range of conditions that affect the liver's structure
and function, leading to potential liver damage.It can be caused by various factors,
including viral infections (like hepatitis), excessive alcohol consumption,
fatty liver disease, and genetics.

Symptoms of Liver Disease:

  • Jaundice
  • Fatigue
  • Abdominal Pain
  • Swelling and Fluid Retention
  • Nausea and Vomiting

Kidney Disease

Kidney disease encompasses various conditions affecting kidney function,
which can lead to waste buildup and electrolyte imbalances.
Treatment may involve interventions like dialysis or
transplantation in severe cases.

Symptoms of Kidney Disease:

  • Fatigue and weakness.
  • Swelling in the legs, ankles, feet, or face (edema).
  • Shortness of breath.
  • Persistent itching.
  • Foamy or bloody urine.
